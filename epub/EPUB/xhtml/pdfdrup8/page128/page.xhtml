Punctuation Rules

Commas 
We use commas to:

separate three or more items in a list.

I need bananas, apples, eggs and milk.

separate two complete sentences joined by conjunctions and, but, or, yet.

I want to buy a new car, but I don’t have enough money.

Clarissa was swimming in the pool, and Pete was lying on the beach.

I can’t decide if I want to visit the zoo, or if I want to visit the museum.

separate a list of three or more verbs.

The students in the band march, play their instruments, and wave to the people.

separate parts of dates.

Monday, August 22

, 2017

Period 
We use periods to:

mark the end of a sentence.

Lucy went to the cinema with her friends yesterday evening.

after abbreviations:

Mr. / Mrs. / Ms. / Sun. / Jan. / A.M. / P.M. / B.C.

Question mark 
It is used after a direct question.

What do you want to do this weekend?

Exclamation  
It is used at the end of a sentence that expresses surprise, a strong feeling,  

mark 
or a command.

Be quiet!

Wow! What a surprise!

I can’t believe you are here!
